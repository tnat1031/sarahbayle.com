I have excelled at project management because I understand its foundation:

  1. Effective communication—with key stakeholders such as vendors, coworkers, and customers
  2. Scheduling—setting ambitious yet attainable goals for milestones and meeting/exceeding them
  3. Cost containment—producing the best quality product possible within or under the budget
  4. Prioritization—balancing competing demands and assessing importance of tasks

Jones & Bartlett Learning

Dates: 2007-2010; January, 2014-present

Positions: Production Editor/Freelancer Liaison

I am responsible for coordinating production and timing the delivery of assets that comprise Jones & Bartlett's full curriculum solutions.

Our digital offerings present the biggest project management challenges because they are the newest products. We are constantly refining our processes and products to become more efficient and responsive to the market.

Tasks associated with overseeing digital asset production include:

  • Hiring and managing assessment authors and copyeditors
  • Working in a proprietary CMS
  • Gathering course assets (e.g., slide decks, lecture outlines, discussion questions, animations, ebook files, etc.) and overseeing integration into Moodle
  • Initiating course builds by liaising with solutions analysts
  • Alpha and beta testing courses before release

Example: I recently managed the production of the fifth edition of Health Policy and Politics by Jeri A. Milstead, a key title on the nursing list. I am also overseeing the creation of the companion Navigate 2 course, which goes live in mid-January.

Pearson Learning Solutions

Dates: 2010-2012

Positions: Development Editor, Project Manager

At PLS, I was responsible for producing customized texts for professors and schools. My group specialized in the professional market, meeting the needs of career/trade schools and professional organizations.

The major challenges of the role were the high volume and the tight deadlines. I handled products from the moment they were submitted by field representatives through to fulfillment of customer orders.

In my role:

  • I served as the in-house project lead
  • I reviewed specs for all incoming projects against profit margins
  • I worked with field representatives and customers (professors and schools) to adjust products
  • I collaborated with other in-house departments and outside vendors to deliver on time and within the budget
